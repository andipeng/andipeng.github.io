PhD Student

MIT CSAIL

Hi there!

I am researching away at MIT, where I am advised by Pulkit Agrawal and call CSAIL Embodied Intelligence home. I previously spent two lovely years as an AI Resident with Ece Kamar and the Adaptive Systems and Interaction Group at Microsoft Research. I received a B.S. in Cognitive Science and B.A. in Global Affairs from Yale, where I got my start on playing with robots with Brian Scassellati.

I’m interested in how adaptive learning emerges. By incorporating the principles behind how humans so effortlessly plan and reason in shifting environments, I hope to build artificial agents that learn to adapt quickly and continuously. I find most of my inspiration from the reinforcement learning, HRI, and cognitive science communities.

I also find myself growing ever more concerned that the world isn’t using AI safely, fairly, or ethically. To that end, I've worked at the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) and with the Partnership on AI on understanding the impacts of technology.

The best way to reach me is andipeng [at] mit [dot] edu. Preferred subject line: Your cat is dope.
